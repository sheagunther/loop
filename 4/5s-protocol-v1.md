# 5S — Five Sentence Compression Protocol v1
## Loop MMT™ · 13 April 2026
### Produced by The Forge (compression clause)

---

## About This Document

Five sentences. That's what comes out. Anything goes in — a protocol, a session transcript, a product spec, a stack of ten documents, a philosophical argument, a life story. Five sentences come out. Every sentence load-bearing. No throat-clearing, no hedging, no "in summary." The compression is the product.

**Provenance:** Operator directive, this session. Forged under compression clause.

**FBD Classification:** FBD-5S series.

---

## Section 1 — Scope

**5S compresses any input into exactly five sentences.** Not approximately five. Not a short paragraph. Five sentences, each carrying maximum information density for the input material.

**5S applies to:**
- Any document, document stack, concept, protocol, session, or body of knowledge
- Recursive compression: 5S applied to its own output
- Merged inputs: multiple subjects combined before compression
- Inline protocol composition: 5S as a terminal step after any other protocol's output

**5S does not govern:**
- What to compress (the operator or invoking protocol chooses the input)
- How the input was produced (5S is format-agnostic — it compresses whatever arrives)

---

## Section 2 — Trigger

**Operator-invoked:** "5S this." "Five sentences." "5S([input])."

**Method-invoked:** Any protocol or Work Order that specifies 5S as a terminal compression step.

**Recursive:** "5S(5S([input]))" — compress the input, then compress the compression. Each recursion level loses detail and gains altitude. The operator controls recursion depth.

**Merge:** "5S(merge([input1], [input2]))" — combine two or more subjects into a single five-sentence synthesis. The merge is not concatenation — it's integration. The five sentences describe the combined landscape, not three sentences about one and two about the other.

---

## Section 3 — Procedure

### Step 1 — Read Everything

Ingest the full input. All of it. 5S does not sample — it reads the complete input before compressing. The compression quality is proportional to comprehension depth.

### Step 2 — Identify the Load-Bearing Structure

Before writing, determine: what are the five things that, if the reader knew only these, they would have the highest-fidelity understanding of the input possible in five sentences? This is not summarization (which preserves narrative arc). This is compression (which preserves information density).

The five sentences should cover:
- **What it is** (identity/definition)
- **What it does / why it matters** (function/stakes)
- **How it works** (mechanism — if applicable)
- **What's surprising or non-obvious** (the part the reader wouldn't guess)
- **What follows from it** (implication/next step)

This is a heuristic, not a template. Some inputs are better served by five different dimensions. The operator of 5S is the sentence selection function — choose the five that preserve the most signal.

### Step 3 — Write Five Sentences

Each sentence:
- Is a complete, standalone claim
- Carries information that no other sentence in the set carries (zero redundancy)
- Uses concrete language (no abstractions that could mean anything)
- Earns every word (if a word can be cut without losing meaning, cut it)

No preamble. No "In summary" or "Here are five sentences about X." The five sentences ARE the output. Nothing else.

### Step 4 — Verify

Count. Is it five? Not four. Not six. Five.

Check: does any sentence overlap with another? If yes, merge them and find a new fifth dimension.

Check: could someone who reads only these five sentences make a correct decision about the input material? If not, the wrong five dimensions were chosen. Revise.

---

## Section 4 — Recursion

5S(5S(x)) is valid. Each recursion level compresses further:

- **Depth 1:** 5S(x) — five sentences capturing the full input.
- **Depth 2:** 5S(5S(x)) — five sentences capturing the five sentences. This is altitude gain — the output reads like a description from orbit. Detail is gone; shape remains.
- **Depth 3:** 5S(5S(5S(x))) — five sentences capturing the shape. This approaches a tagline. Each sentence is a thesis statement.
- **Depth 4+:** Theoretical. At this depth, each sentence is an axiom. Useful for testing whether the input has irreducible principles.

At each recursion level, the input to 5S is ONLY the output of the prior level. The original material is not re-read. This is the integrity mechanism — each compression stands on the prior compression, not on privileged access to the source.

---

## Section 5 — Merge

5S(merge(a, b)) compresses two or more inputs into a single five-sentence synthesis.

**Merge rules:**
- The five sentences describe the integrated landscape, not the individual inputs. "Subject A does X; Subject B does Y" is concatenation, not merge.
- Prioritize: where do the inputs intersect? Where do they collide? Where does one illuminate the other? The merge surfaces the relationship, not just the contents.
- If the inputs have nothing in common, say so in sentence one, then give two sentences to each. That's the honest merge of unrelated material.

## Section 6 — Composition

5S composes with any protocol in the Loop MMT registry:

| Pattern | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| `5S(RCR output)` | Compress a full RCR resolution into five sentences |
| `5S(Parallax result)` | Compress a Parallax comparison into five sentences |
| `5S(merge(KP1, KP2))` | Synthesize two Knowledge Packs into five sentences |
| `5S(session)` | Compress an entire session's work into five sentences |
| `5S(EOD handoff)` | Five-sentence state for quick operator briefing |
| `RCR → 5S` | Run an RCR, then compress the resolution |
| `5S → Stake` | Compress, then stake a position on the compression |

5S is a terminal compressor. It takes any input and produces fixed-format output. This makes it the universal adapter — any protocol's output can be 5S'd for rapid transmission.

## Section 7 — FBD Controls

| ID | Name | Rule |
|----|------|------|
| FBD-5S1 | Exact count | Output is exactly five sentences. Not four, not six. Count before delivering. |
| FBD-5S2 | Zero redundancy | No sentence overlaps with another. Each carries unique information. If overlap is detected, merge and find a new dimension. |
| FBD-5S3 | No framing | No preamble, no "here's the summary," no closing. The five sentences are the complete output. |
| FBD-5S4 | Recursion integrity | At each recursion level, only the prior level's output is the input. No re-reading the original source. The compression must stand on itself. |
| FBD-5S5 | Merge, don't concatenate | 5S(merge(a,b)) produces integration, not two mini-summaries side by side. |

---

## Section 8 — Sequence Position

5S activates on demand. No fixed position in session flow. Most common positions: end-of-session compression (5S the session), end-of-protocol compression (5S the output), and cross-session transmission (5S a handoff for quick reconstruction).

---

## Section 9 — Four Corners

- **FBD:** Exact count, zero redundancy, no framing, recursion integrity, merge rules. Five controls for five sentences.
- **FWW(C):** 5S. Two characters. Says exactly what it does. The constraint IS the engagement — five sentences to capture anything is a game, and games produce focus.
- **STP:** The output is self-evidently verifiable. Count the sentences. Did each one teach you something? Trust is built in ten seconds of reading.
- **SNR:** Maximum SNR protocol in the registry. The entire point is signal-to-noise optimization. If 5S has low SNR, the methodology has a contradiction.
