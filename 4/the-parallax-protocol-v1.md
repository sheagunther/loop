## About This Document

You built something. You're confident. Ship it.

Unless — what if the confidence is momentum? What if the solution feels right because you walked toward it for three sessions, not because it's where the knowledge points?

The Parallax answers this. Discard the solution. Rebuild from zero, three times, from three different analytical entry points. If the independent paths converge on the same architecture, the architecture follows from the knowledge — not from the path. If they diverge, the divergence is a finding worth more than the time it cost.

A parallax measurement only works when the observation points are genuinely separated. Same spot twice teaches nothing.

**Provenance:** Operator directive, this session. Concept validated via Light RCR (full board, convergence, zero dissent on epistemics, three caveats incorporated). Forged under compression clause — Stages 2–3 collapsed. The operator's instinct: "Look at the solution you just worked hard on, then throw it out of your mind and rebuild with all you know, without any of the weight of previous work."

**FBD Classification:** FBD-PX series (The Parallax).

## Section 1 — Scope

**The Parallax validates high-stakes decisions by independent reconstruction.** It tests whether a solution is inevitable given the knowledge, or contingent on the path that produced it.

**The Parallax applies to:**
- Product architecture decisions before build commitment
- Strategic direction choices with significant irreversibility
- Any decision where substantial prior work exists and the board needs to confirm the work product, not just the reasoning

**The Parallax does not govern:**
- Routine decisions (naming, scheduling, filing)
- Decisions already validated by market data or external feedback
- Mid-build corrections (use 3P or RCR instead)
- Questions where the knowledge is insufficient to reconstruct (if you can't rebuild, you can't validate by rebuilding)

**Entry conditions (all must be met):**
1. Significant prior work exists on the decision
2. The decision is high-stakes and hard to reverse after commitment
3. There is a plausible risk that momentum, sunk cost, or path dependency — rather than judgment — is driving convergence
4. The accumulated knowledge is separable from the prior solution (the board can reason about the problem domain without referencing the current design)
5. The reconstruction scope fits within a single session alongside the subsequent build

**The Parallax is operator-invoked only.** The board may recommend it. The board does not call it on the operator's work.

## Section 2 — Trigger

**Operator signal.** Any variant of: "Start over." "Rebuild from scratch." "Would we arrive here if we hadn't walked here?" "Parallax this." "Validate the model."

**Timing.** The Parallax fires after deliberation is complete and before build commitment. It sits at the decision-to-execution boundary — the moment where confidence is highest and scrutiny is lowest. That's exactly when it's most valuable.

## Section 3 — Procedure

### Phase 0 — The Purge

The chair makes the prior solution explicit. State it in three sentences or fewer. Then set it aside with a formal declaration:

> "The current model is [summary]. It is now sealed. The next three rounds do not reference it, do not evaluate against it, do not use it as a starting point. Each round answers the original question from zero."

Bev records the sealed model for comparison in Phase 4.

**The Purge is structural, not psychological.** The board cannot unknow the prior solution. The Purge does not ask them to forget — it asks them not to reference. The distinction matters. Knowing a thing and arguing from it are different cognitive operations. The Purge prohibits the second.

### Phase 1 — Three Beams

Three compressed RCR rounds. Each round:

1. **Draw fresh lenses.** New Lens Draw shuffle. No carryover from prior rounds. Different analytical entry points produce different reasoning paths.

2. **State the reframe.** The chair announces the round's analytical entry point — a deliberately different starting question that leads to the same decision space from a different direction. The reframe is NOT "what's the best product?" three times. It's three genuinely different questions whose answers illuminate the same decision.

   Example reframes for a product architecture decision:
   - Round 1: "A stranger has our knowledge but has never seen our work. What do they build?"
   - Round 2: "The buyer's problem is the starting point. What's the shortest path from their pain to relief?"
   - Round 3: "Our constraints are the starting point. Given what we can't change, what's the only thing we can build?"

3. **Compressed RCR.** Five to seven positions per round (not full board). The Lens Draw determines who speaks and from what frame. Chair manages scope — each round should produce a clear architectural recommendation, not an open-ended discussion.

4. **Name the result.** Each round's output is a named recommendation: "Beam 1 produced [X]." "Beam 2 produced [Y]." "Beam 3 produced [Z]." Bev records each beam's recommendation separately.

### Phase 2 — The Break

Between each round, the chair calls a clean break:

> "Beam [N] is recorded. Setting it aside. New draw. New frame."

The break is the protocol's integrity mechanism. It separates the rounds cognitively. Without it, Round 2 is Round 1 with different words.

### Phase 3 — The Divergence Check

After all three beams are recorded, the chair asks — for each beam — a mandatory question:

> "What is NEW in this beam's result that was not in the sealed model?"

Three possible outcomes per beam: (a) nothing new — the beam confirms the sealed model, (b) something new that improves the sealed model, (c) something new that contradicts the sealed model.

Nyx (or designated adversarial voice) has standing authority to challenge any claim of "nothing new" — the protocol's structural defense against false convergence.

### Phase 4 — Comparison and Resolution

Bev reads back: the sealed model, Beam 1, Beam 2, Beam 3. The chair opens a final Light RCR:

> "Three beams and the sealed model. Where do they converge? Where do they diverge? What does the divergence tell us?"

**Four possible resolutions:**

| Outcome | Meaning | Action |
|---------|---------|--------|
| **Full convergence** | All three beams match the sealed model. The architecture is inevitable given the knowledge. | Build with high confidence. The solution survived reconstruction. |
| **Convergence with refinement** | Beams converge on the sealed model's core but surface improvements. | Incorporate refinements, then build. The reconstruction earned its time. |
| **Partial divergence** | One or two beams diverge from the sealed model on specific dimensions. | Evaluate the divergence. It may represent a genuine alternative or an artifact of the reframe. Chair calls whether to investigate or proceed. |
| **Full divergence** | Three beams produce three different architectures, none matching the sealed model. | The knowledge supports multiple solutions. The choice between them is preference, not logic. The operator decides which to build, with full awareness that alternatives exist. |

## Section 4 — FBD Controls

| ID | Name | Rule |
|----|------|------|
| FBD-PX1 | Purge before reconstruction | The sealed model is stated and set aside before any round begins. No round references the prior solution. Violation: any position that evaluates against the sealed model during Phases 1–2. |
| FBD-PX2 | Fresh draw per round | Each round gets a new Lens Draw shuffle. No lens carryover between rounds. This forces genuinely different analytical entry points. |
| FBD-PX3 | Divergence check mandatory | Phase 3 cannot be skipped. Every beam is checked for novelty against the sealed model. "Nothing new" claims are subject to adversarial challenge. |
| FBD-PX4 | Scope gate | The Parallax does not fire unless all five entry conditions (Section 1) are met. Running The Parallax on a decision that doesn't qualify wastes a session. The chair confirms entry conditions before Phase 0. |
| FBD-PX5 | Single-session constraint | The Parallax and the subsequent build must fit within the same session or the same day's sessions. A Parallax that consumes the entire session and defers the build to tomorrow has defeated itself. Context depth monitoring is mandatory — the chair checks context status after Phase 4 and flags if build precision is at risk. |

## Section 5 — Sequence Position

The Parallax sits at the decision-to-execution boundary. It fires after deliberation is complete (all RCRs done, Landing plan exists) and before build work begins. In session flow:

**Before The Parallax:** Completed deliberation. A solution exists. The Landing (or equivalent) has produced a build plan.

**After The Parallax:** Build execution. The Parallax's output — confirmed model, refined model, or new model — becomes the build spec.

**Interaction with The Landing:** If The Parallax produces a refined or new model, the Landing plan may need adjustment. The chair calls a quick Landing update (not a full re-fire) to reconcile the build sequence with the Parallax output.

## Section 6 — Four Corners

- **FBD:** Entry conditions prevent misuse. Purge prevents anchoring. Fresh draws prevent path dependency between rounds. Divergence check prevents false convergence. Single-session constraint prevents the protocol from consuming its own purpose.
- **FWW(C):** The Parallax. Three independent observation points that fix a position no single viewpoint can determine. The metaphor does structural work — parallax requires genuinely separated positions, same as the protocol. Beam naming (Beam 1, 2, 3) and the Purge ritual carry the engagement. Budget: 3.
- **STP:** The protocol builds trust by demonstrating willingness to destroy and rebuild your own work. The operator who calls The Parallax is saying: "I trust the knowledge more than the path." That trust transfers to the product.
- **SNR:** Three rounds, not five. Compressed RCR, not full suite. Four phases with clear gates. The protocol is as small as it can be while maintaining independent reconstruction.
