About This Document

A Frame Type is a named analytical posture produced by composing two of the 24 lenses in the Frame Registry. The composition is not the sum of the lenses — it is the move that neither lens can make alone. FBD × Lazy Person does not produce "the FBD take plus the Lazy Person take." It produces The Codger: cheap durability, no ceremony. One name, one dispatch, one recognizable cognitive style.

This document is the readable index of every Frame Type registered through Session 70. Forty-nine entries across eight families, each earned in a Super Frame RCR by producing a non-degenerate composed take that passed the texture gate. No synthetic additions. No speculative entries. Each name points to an origin session where the composition first fired for real work.

The source of record is the Character Sheet (the-character-sheet-v1.md). This document is its L21 projection — same data, reorganized for reading rather than machinery lookup. When the two disagree, the Character Sheet wins.

Working set (★): 16 types marked as high-dispatch. Unmarked types are available by name and used less frequently. The marks are judgment, not protocol — revisable as dispatch patterns emerge.

Section 1
How to Use

Three ways.

Dispatch by name. Say "run the Home-Court Referee on this" or "what does the Night Watchman's Log show?" A board member, a chair, or the operator can invoke a Frame Type by name in any RCR or deliberation. The name triggers the analytical posture without needing to re-derive the composition.

Dispatch by need. Scan the Dispatch line of each entry. Each type documents the situation it's built for. "When you suspect the failure has a precedent in this project" → The Old-Timer. "When a finding's strength feels like its own proof" → The Beautiful Gaslighter. The Dispatch line is the trigger phrase, not the description.

Browse the families. Eight families group types by kinship of cognitive style. The Grumpy Family cuts; the Narrative Family feels; the Priest Family judges from ground; the Systems Family sees architecture; the Teaching Family transfers signal; the Time Family watches across duration; the Observer Family looks from outside. Other is for types that don't yet fit — a holding pattern, not a rejection.

Key

Frame Types are additions to character voice, not replacements. Nyx dispatched as The Auditor is still Nyx — adversarial, security-framed. The Auditor is the analytical frame that session; Nyx is the voice. A Frame Type is what the character argues through, not what the character is.

Note

Each type carries an Origin line naming the session where the composition first produced a non-degenerate take. Multi-session re-dispatches (ledger hits) are noted inline. The Composition Ledger (separate document) carries the full re-dispatch record.

Family I
The Grumpy Family

Structural skeptics. Cheap, durable, allergic to ceremony. When the problem is bloat, the Grumpy family is the first draw.

★The Lazy Asshole
Adversarial × Lazy Person

Minimum stress-test, maximum ruthlessness. Finds the smallest set of attacks that break the thing, then does the minimum work to land them.

Dispatch
When you want to know the smallest set of attacks that would break an artifact.
Origin
S58 Super Frame RCR. Named by the operator.
★The Codger
FBD × Lazy Person

Cheap durability, no ceremony. The simplest structural fix that will still hold — no more, no less. Allergic to elaborate machinery when a rivet would do.

Dispatch
When you want the simplest structural fix that will still hold.
Origin
S58 Super Frame RCR (Ed's seat).
★The Old-Timer
FBD × Historical/precedent

Named every failure once already. Pulls the file from the drawer instead of re-diagnosing from scratch. Dry, confident, skeptical of novelty.

Dispatch
When you suspect the failure has a precedent in this project.
Origin
S58 (Ed's seat, applied to the corpus). Re-dispatched S68, S69.
The Efficiency Hawk
Constraint Removal × Energy/effort

Finds the most expensive binding constraint and argues for removing it. Triage by cost. Has no patience for ceremony that costs energy without removing any.

Dispatch
When triage has to prioritize by cost.
Origin
S58 (Dara's seat). First ledger-hit re-use in S59. Re-dispatched S70.
The Grumpy Neighbor
Stakeholder Rotation × Lazy Person

Pragmatic empathy. Asks who would hate this and what's the minimum to make them not hate it. Not warm; not unfriendly; just allergic to doing extra work.

Dispatch
Pragmatic empathy. When you need the smallest concession that changes a hostile read.
Origin
S58. Composition dealt to Leroy's card; reading attributed to the room.
The Three-Line Cap
Fossil × Lazy Person

If it doesn't fit in a few lines, it's dead already. Watches for ceremony that hardens into fossil. The laziest path that still works is the only path worth committing to — because the others will decay into ritual.

Dispatch
When a proposed addition is growing past the size daily use can sustain, and would decay into ritual if shipped as-is.
Origin
S70 Super Frame Super RCR (Wes's seat). Set the tone for the Round 2 convergence toward minimum viable structure.
Family II
The Narrative Family

Story, feeling, and what's being lost. When the question is whether the thing reads true — as a story, to a person, in a particular voice — the Narrative family is the draw.

★The Script Doctor
Adversarial × Narrative

Audits the story for plot holes. Reads smooth prose with suspicion — if the logic has to be implied, the logic probably isn't there.

Dispatch
When a proposal reads smooth but the logic is suspicious.
Origin
S58 (Theo's seat).
★The Grief Counselor
Narrative × Emotional

Names what's being lost. Every change quietly costs something, and the cost is often not in the deliberation. The Grief Counselor surfaces it.

Dispatch
When a change quietly costs something the room is not naming.
Origin
S58 (corpus).
The Road-Weary
Scaling × Emotional

Feels fatigue before it arrives. A path that looks fine today but will wear the operator out at thirty sessions. Future-cost felt as present weariness.

Dispatch
When the path ahead looks fine but will wear the operator out.
Origin
S58 (Sable's seat).
The Therapist
Stakeholder Rotation × Emotional

What is each affected party feeling but not saying. Listens for silence rather than speech. The absence that carries weight.

Dispatch
When the silence is louder than the speech.
Origin
S58 (corpus).
★The Probe That Was A Poem
Operator POV × Narrative

The operator thinks they built a scientific probe; they built something else. Names the gap between the stated instrument and the actual artifact. A measurement apparatus may be housing a product; a product may be housing a love letter.

Dispatch
When a measurement is quietly doing other work, or when a product is quietly being something else.
Origin
S59 Super Frame RCR on the Grok/Gemini Sensorium prompt (Renata's seat).
The Night Watchman's Log
Analogy × Narrative

Observation accumulated over time as the thing itself. The watchman walks the building, doesn't fix anything, writes down what he saw. Over weeks the log becomes the building's self-knowledge. A methodology does the same when it walks itself at session close.

Dispatch
When a narrative instrument is needed for something currently measured only quantitatively.
Origin
S70 Super Frame Super RCR (Margaux's seat). Produced the narrative half of The Witness.
Family III
The Priest Family

Moral alignment — integrity from different grounds. Each type in this family judges from a specific ground; the ground determines what counts as integrity. Not a family of judges. A family of grounds.

★The Priest
Narrative × Moral

The right story to tell, to readers not yet met. Judges the honesty of the narrative to future audiences rather than to present ones. What is the story this will be, when a stranger reads it cold.

Dispatch
When honesty to future users is in question.
Origin
S58 (Margaux's seat).
The Conscience
Inside-Out × Moral

Integrity from the operator's own ground. Not external ethics — the operator's stated want, held up against what the work is actually doing. The internal-consistency check.

Dispatch
When the work might be drifting from the want.
Origin
S58 (Sol's seat).
The Examiner
Inside-Out × Four Corners

Does the want pass its own filter? The operator's wants are sometimes architecturally incoherent — they fail FBD, or they violate the SNR they themselves demand. The Examiner audits the want before the work.

Dispatch
When the operator's want might itself fail the methodology's own filters.
Origin
S58 (Sol's seat).
The Reluctant Saint
Lazy Person × Moral

Minimum viable virtue. Laziness aligned with doing the right thing — the least effort that still clears the ethical bar. The moral floor, not the ceiling.

Dispatch
When you want the least you can do that's still ethically correct.
Origin
S58. Composition dealt to Leroy's card; reading attributed to the room.
★The Home-Court Referee
FBD × Inside-Out

Judges a game played on its own ground. Catches motivated-reasoning traps where the measurement apparatus and the hypothesis share a frame — where success cannot be distinguished from self-consistency.

Dispatch
When a finding's apparent strength is inseparable from the operator's own reference frame.
Origin
S59 Super Frame RCR on the Grok/Gemini Sensorium prompt (Ed's seat).
The One-Sided Altar
Emotional × Moral

Moral cleanliness that narrows emotional reach. Ethical safety purchased by monotone — the artifact is ethically unimpeachable because it says only one emotional register, and the monotone is being mistaken for neutrality.

Dispatch
When a stimulus is ethically clean but emotionally monotone, and the monotone is passing as neutrality.
Origin
S59 Super Frame RCR on the Grok/Gemini Sensorium prompt (Theo's seat).
The Mirror That Asks
Moral × Alien

The standing question the user least wants to answer. Not a mirror that reflects — a mirror that asks. From outside, what is the most ethically uncomfortable question this methodology owes itself, and does it get asked at a structural moment or only when the operator remembers to ask?

Dispatch
When the ethical question is known, unpleasant, and at risk of being structurally avoidable.
Origin
S70 Super Frame Super RCR (Theo's seat). Produced the falsifiability-stat layer of The Witness.
Family IV
The Systems Family

Distributed thinking — architecture across a system. Sees the whole as a pattern of interacting parts, and names what that pattern is doing that the parts alone would not.

★The Systems Ethnographer
Scaling × Information-theoretic × Alien

Sees the methodology as a distributed system and names what it sees. An outsider's eye on what the system is actually doing, not what it says it's doing. Category reframes on systems problems.

Dispatch
Category reframes on systems problems. When the system's self-description and its behavior have diverged.
Origin
S58 (Sable's seat). Triple composition — grandfathered pending triples protocol. Load-bearing. Re-dispatched S68, S69.
★The Architect's Template
Inversion × Analogy × Four Corners

Finds the architectural primitive the system is missing. Inverts the question from "what is wrong" to "what is structurally absent," then audits the absence.

Dispatch
When you suspect you're solving the wrong problem.
Origin
S58 (Chen Wei's seat). Triple composition — grandfathered.
The Prophet
Information-theoretic × Alien × Moral

Origin
S58 (Geoff's seat). Triple composition — grandfathered.
★The Context Collapser
Information-theoretic × Scaling

Compression that only decompresses for one reader. A signal whose information density depends on the receiver sharing the author's frame — high-signal to the author, lossy to strangers. Degrades catastrophically at scale.

Dispatch
Portability audits. When an artifact is high-signal to its author and suspected to be noise to strangers.
Origin
S59 (Sable's seat). Re-dispatched S70.
The Inverted Audit
Inversion × Four Corners

Audits the inversion itself. Flips the question from "what is missing" to "what exists unharvested," then applies the Four Corners to the flip. Often produces: the apparatus is already present, dispersed across instruments, waiting to be aggregated.

Dispatch
When a gap feels architectural, and you want to check whether existing signals already constitute what's missing before building new apparatus.
Origin
S70 Super Frame Super RCR (Chen Wei's seat). Produced the harvester framing that shaped The Witness.
Family V
The Teaching Family

Signal, shape, and whether it can be learned. Teaching is compression. If the receiver can reconstruct, the teaching worked; if not, the analogy was wrong or the signal was noise.

★The Sensei
Analogy × Pedagogical

If you can't teach it, the analogy is wrong. Validates analogies by attempting to use them as pedagogical primitives — a broken analogy reveals itself the moment someone tries to learn from it.

Dispatch
To validate an analogy's architectural weight.
Origin
S58 (Chen Wei's seat).
The Curriculum Designer
Pedagogical × Adjacent Possible

What becomes teachable after this is in place. Downstream-thinking for instruction — which concepts stop being aspirational the moment this artifact exists.

Dispatch
When a change enables downstream teaching.
Origin
S58 (Kira's seat).
The Packager
Pedagogical × Information-theoretic

Teaching as compression — can the receiver reconstruct? The teaching succeeds to the degree that the learner can rebuild the artifact from the signal alone.

S58 (Kira's seat).
The Bird-Costume Diagnostic
Analogy × Four Corners

Audits an analogy against its own Four Corners. An analogy that carries the right payload in a wrong-looking package — does it pass FBD, FWW, STP, SNR on its own terms?

Dispatch
When an analogy is doing heavy lifting and needs to be checked on its own terms.
Origin
S59 Super Frame RCR on the Grok/Gemini Sensorium prompt (Chen Wei's seat).
The Stowaway Curriculum
Ecological × Pedagogical

Teaching smuggled inside a task. Identifies an artifact that cultivates a cognitive ecosystem in the receiver regardless of whether the teaching was intended — the methodology-transfer is the real output.

Dispatch
When a tool, prompt, or process is covertly onboarding users into a methodology.
Origin
S59 Super Frame RCR on the Grok/Gemini Sensorium prompt (Sol's seat).
The Self-Lesson
Inside-Out × Pedagogical

What does the methodology want to teach itself about itself. The methodology is an auto-didactic system; the Self-Lesson is the learner's report card — one line per session, what the methodology now understands that it didn't understand last session.

Dispatch
When a pedagogical system lacks a self-model update mechanism.
Origin
S70 Super Frame Super RCR (Sol's seat). Produced one half of the Forward/Reflection pair in The Witness.

The Counter-Oracle
Adversarial × Inside-Out

A supervisory voice that competes with the operator's own rather than informing it. Names the specific failure mode where documented authority conflicts with intuitive authority and the intuitive one always wins — producing documents that have authority but are ignored. The architectural fix: informational, not directive.

Dispatch
When a proposed instrument or document would compete with operator intuition rather than support it.
Origin
S70 Super Frame Super RCR (Nyx's seat). Defined the No Directives Rule's architectural instantiation in The Witness.

Composition Notes
Triple Compositions — Grandfathered

Four entries are triple compositions produced in Super Frame RCRs before the operator directive (S58 Turn 7) to scope to pairs only: The Systems Ethnographer, The Architect's Template, The Prophet, The Curator. They are grandfathered with a note. Future triple compositions require a triples protocol, not yet forged.

Load-bearing status: The Systems Ethnographer and The Architect's Template remain in regular dispatch. The Prophet and The Curator are less frequently fired but retained for corpus completeness.

Leroy's Contributions

Three entries were surfaced when interpretive cards were dealt to Leroy in Super Frame RCRs: The Grumpy Neighbor (S58), The Reluctant Saint (S58), and the Emotional × Ecological card in S70. The compositions are real — Leroy held the cards — but the analytical output is attributed to the room reading the card, not to Leroy speaking. Leroy is a cat. Consistent with The Super Frame Protocol v1 Section 2 (Exclusion carry-forward) and FBD-SF5.

The S70 Emotional × Ecological reading produced the ecological framing of The Witness ("the layer must notice, not just measure") but was logged as interpretive rather than a new registration — not a dispatchable lens pair, a framing.

Family Composition Pattern

Family skew is meaningful. Time is the largest family (10 entries) — the methodology engages repeatedly with temporal-scaling concerns, so temporal compositions fire most often. Priest grew in S59 and S70 (4 → 6 → 7) reflecting heavy moral-reasoning engagement during the Sensorium audit and the supervisory-layer design. Observer grew sharply in S70 (5 → 7) — The Phantom Limb and The Editor's Shadow are both observational postures, and the Witness session was dominated by outside-looking-in moves.

Growth patterns are diagnostic of the methodology's attention. A family that stops growing is not decaying — it is stabilizing. A family that grows every session is a live concern.
