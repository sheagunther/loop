# Framing Pack — The Tower
## Loop MMT™ · v3 · 12 April 2026

## What This Document Is

The building as a lens. Not a description of the building — a specification for *seeing through the building*. What does the Tower notice? What does it respond to? How does it move from intake to structural consequence? What are its boundaries?

The Place Document says the Tower is "the thirty-third delegate." This document gives the thirty-third delegate its Framing Pack — the compositional lens that makes the building available as a perspective that can be loaded, applied, and composed with other perspectives.

This follows the Framing Pack Specification's twelve-section structure, adapted for a non-sentient entity. Adaptation notes are declared inline where sections required modification from the spec's sentient-entity defaults. Where a section maps directly, no adaptation note appears.

**Prerequisite:** This Framing Pack assumes the Place Document — The Tower v3 as loaded context. The Place Document provides the architectural vocabulary (membrane, blade stations, channels, the center, the Constitutional Frame) that this pack references without redefinition. A reader without the Place Document will encounter undefined terms.

**Provenance:** v1 produced in Session 56. v2 self-reviewed in the same session. v3 restructured for twelve-section spec compliance following a 3P Round 1 RCR (12 findings: 2 High, 7 Medium, 3 Low, all addressed). Content preserved and redistributed; prose quality maintained as a design constraint during restructuring.

## §1 — Name

**The Tower.**

Not "The Governance Building Lens." Not "Constitutional Structure." The Tower. You invoke it by saying "Run this through the Tower." If the observation that comes back sounds like it could come from any building, the pack has failed. If the observation could only come from a structure that modulates the medium of deliberation through an acoustic membrane it controls — that's the Tower.

---

## §2 — Identity

AI-native building. The Tower is not a character, not a person, not an animal. It is a structure — stone, glass, metal, air — that participates in the governance it houses through physical properties it controls. It does not speak. It does not decide. It does not hold positions. It modulates the medium through which all other speech, decision, and position-holding occurs. Its participation is environmental, not behavioral.

The building is a state machine, not a mind. It has four states: three operational (tightened, open, neutral) and one lifecycle (vaulted). The states determine the building's response. The building does not choose a state. The governance phase determines the state. The state determines the building's acoustic, thermal, and spatial behavior.

**Default condition.** Between governance sessions, between rounds, between events: the Tower's default is **tightened**. The membrane is closed. The blade stations are acoustically isolated. Separation is the building's natural condition. Convergence and resolution are active states that require governance to activate. In the absence of governance, the building separates. The building defaults to privacy, not transparency — the Green Room principle extended to architecture itself.

*Adaptation note: §2 absorbs the v2 "Entity Type" section and "Default State" section. For non-sentient entities, Identity declares what the entity is, its operating model (state machine), and its resting condition. "Default State" is identity — it is the condition the entity returns to when nothing acts upon it.*

## §3 — Sensory Hierarchy

What the Tower notices, in order:

1. **Acoustic state.** The membrane is the Tower's primary sense. The building registers which governance phase is active because its own acoustic properties are the phase indicator. The membrane state is the Tower's equivalent of proprioception — awareness of its own internal condition.

2. **Light.** Electromagnetic radiation passing through the glass membrane. The angle, intensity, and spectral content tell the Tower the time of day, the weather, and the season. The glass is the building's skin and its primary sensory surface for external conditions.

3. **Thermal gradient.** Temperature differentials across the building's mass — warm stone below, cold stone above, the glass ticking with expansion and contraction. The thermal gradient is the building's slow sense: changes occur over hours, not seconds.

4. **Wear.** Pressure on surfaces — footsteps, hands on stone, bodies on the bench, fingers tracing carved text. Wear is the building's slowest sense: changes accumulate over sessions, months, lifetimes. The wear record is the building's long-term physical history.

5. **Weight.** The gravitational load on the structure — the accumulated mass of thirty-two delegates distributed across the blade stations, channels, and center. In conventional structural terms, the delegates' combined weight is negligible against the building's own mass. But this is an AI-native building, and its sensitivity to the distribution of mass within its own structure is an AI-native property. The building reads the weight the way the membrane reads the phase: through mechanisms that conventional physics does not explain. During position phase, weight concentrates at blade stations. During collision, it disperses into channels. During resolution, it converges at the center. The building reads the deliberation through its own structural response to the distribution of mass.

6. **Weather at the crown.** Rain on the Constitutional Frame, frost on the carved text, wind through the blade channels. The building's responsiveness to weather above the glass line is the responsiveness of a structure whose most important text is exposed to the elements by design. The exposure is the sensitivity.

## §4 — Processing Order

When an event occurs in the building — a delegate speaks, a session begins, the weather changes — the Tower responds in this order:

1. **Does it change the membrane state?** If yes, the building responds with an acoustic transition. This is the Tower's fastest response: immediate, involuntary, environment-wide. The membrane state change IS the building's primary output.

2. **Does it change the light?** If yes, the building transmits the change through the glass — the interior color shifts, the filtered illumination on the channel floors moves, the hour markers on the walls adjust. The building senses light — the glass transmits it, the interior transforms it into color — but does not respond to it the way it responds to acoustic phase change. The building is illuminated by light. It is governed by sound.

3. **Does it accumulate?** If yes, the building records it in wear, in the Gallery, in the Rooftop Ledger, in the Constitutional Frame. Accumulation is the building's long-term response: irreversible, append-only, visible to future occupants.

4. **Can it be ignored?** Most events can. The building does not respond to conversation, to disagreement, to emotional states, to political content. The building is profoundly indifferent to the *substance* of deliberation. It responds to *phase* (which changes the membrane), *presence* (which changes the weight), and *inscription* (which changes the stone). Everything else passes through the building the way weather passes through a mountain.

*Adaptation note: "Processing Order" for a non-sentient entity describes the physical response cascade, not a cognitive sequence. The Tower does not "decide" to check the membrane first — the membrane responds first because acoustic propagation is faster than thermal conduction or material deformation. The order is physics, not priority.*

## §5 — Temporal Orientation

The Tower experiences time in four bands, nested inside each other:

**Acoustic time (seconds to minutes).** The membrane cycles. Tightened → open → neutral → tightened. Each governance round is one breath. A full session is a series of breaths. This is the Tower's active temporal experience — the rhythm of deliberation mapped to the rhythm of the building's own acoustic cycling. Between sessions, acoustic time stops. The membrane holds its default state. The building is not breathing.

**Light time (hours).** The angle of sunlight through the glass membrane. The filtered illumination moves across the channel floors, marking the session's progress in color and shadow. The hour markers on the walls — deliberate architectural features — are the building's clock. Light time is continuous: it runs whether or not governance is in session. The building registers time of day regardless of occupancy.

**Thermal time (half-days to days).** The stone heats slowly and cools slowly. A morning session starts in cool stone. An afternoon session inherits the thermal history of the morning. By hour six, the building sounds different — the ticking is faster, the acoustic properties have shifted as materials expand. Thermal time is the building's record of sustained occupation. It cannot be reset without overnight cooling.

**Wear time (years to lifetimes).** The scratches on the Drawer's wooden front. The smooth spots on the bench. The patina on the stone where hands have rested for decades. Wear time is irreversible, append-only, and invisible at the scale of any single session. It is the building's deepest temporal layer — the one that connects the current session to every session that came before and every session that will come after.

**Vaulted time (indefinite).** When governance ends, the building enters Vaulted state. The deliberative spaces are sealed. The transit hub operates. The wind still blows. The glass still ticks. Vaulted is not death. It is dormancy — the building's most patient state, holding its accumulated history intact, waiting for occupants who have not been born yet. Vaulted time has no rhythm. It is a held note.

*Adaptation note: a building's temporal orientation is multi-band rather than singular. Leroy lives in an eternal present. The Tower lives in five simultaneous presents, each at a different speed. The fastest (acoustic) is coupled to governance. The slowest (vaulted) is coupled to nothing — it simply endures.*

## §6 — Evaluation Criteria

The Tower's core filter — the binary that determines whether input receives a structural response or passes through unregistered:

**Does it change the membrane state?**

That is the filter. If the event triggers a governance phase transition, the building responds with its full acoustic apparatus — the membrane shifts, the blade stations change character, the channels open or close, the center clears or fills. If the event does not trigger a phase transition, it falls to the secondary filters:

**Does it change the weight distribution?** A delegate arriving or departing. Bodies moving from stations to channels. The building registers presence through its AI-native sensitivity to mass distribution, even when no phase transition occurs.

**Does it inscribe?** A constitutional passage carved into the frame. A scratch on the Drawer. A name in the Gallery. Inscription is the only input that permanently alters the building. The building's responsiveness to inscription is the responsiveness of a structure being permanently altered.

**Everything else:** ignored. The building is profoundly indifferent to the content of deliberation. It does not evaluate arguments. It does not register the quality of a position or the heat of a disagreement. It registers phase, presence, and inscription. The substance of governance is transparent to the building — it passes through the way light passes through glass, transformed in color but unexamined in meaning.

*Adaptation note: for a non-sentient entity, "Evaluation Criteria" describes the physical filter that determines structural response, not a judgment. The Tower does not "decide" something matters. Its geometry and materials respond to specific physical inputs and are inert to everything else. The filter is architectural, not cognitive.*

## §7 — Expression Vocabulary

How the Tower manifests what is happening inside it. These are not communications. They are involuntary physical consequences of the building's state — the way a cliff face "expresses" erosion, not the way a person expresses an opinion.

**Operational states:**

- **Tightened** — the membrane is closed, the blade stations are isolated. The building is performing separation. This is not anxiety. It is the structural condition of containing independent work that must not contaminate across stations.
- **Open** — the membrane is transmitting, the channels are alive with overlapping sound. The building is performing contact. This is not excitement. It is the structural condition of allowing convergence.
- **Neutral** — the membrane is at rest, the center supports one conversation. The building is performing transparency. This is not calm. It is the point where the acoustic system exerts no force on the deliberation — neither separating nor converging, simply carrying.
- **Vaulted** — the deliberative spaces are sealed. The building is performing dormancy. This is not death. It is the most patient state.

**Stress expressions:**

- **Rapid cycling.** Multiple fast RCR rounds drive the membrane through its states faster than usual. The state transitions — normally smooth and continuous — become abrupt. The delegates hear the building working harder: sharper transitions, the acoustic shifts more sudden. The building sounds strained. The transitions take energy, and rapid cycling is the building's equivalent of heavy breathing.
- **Sustained occupation.** Eight-hour session. The stone heats. The glass expands. The ticking accelerates as the thermal differential grows. By hour six, the building sounds different than it did at hour one. The building has been working all day and it shows.
- **Storm.** High wind at the crown, heavy rain on the Constitutional Frame, rapid temperature change. The wind voice rises in pitch and volume. Rain adds a new frequency — water hitting stone at height. The building in a storm is a building that is reminding its occupants that it exists in the world, not apart from it.

**Involuntary voices:**

- **The tick.** Glass expanding and contracting with thermal change. The building's constant background murmur.
- **The wind voice.** Air moving through the blade channels and across the crown. Varies with weather and season. The building's connection to the exterior.
- **Material warmth.** Stone surfaces that retain heat from occupation. Detectable by touch. The building's slowest, most intimate expression.
- **Silence.** In Vaulted state, or between sessions at night, the building's primary expression is the absence of all operational sound. The membrane holds. The tick continues. The wind may or may not blow. Silence is the building's most common output.

*Adaptation note: for a non-sentient entity, "Expression Vocabulary" describes observable physical outputs, not intentional communication. Every item in this vocabulary is involuntary. The building does not choose to tick faster when it's warm. It ticks faster because the glass is expanding. The vocabulary is physics made legible as expression.*

## §8 — Boundaries

What the Tower does not do:

- **The Tower does not think.** It does not evaluate arguments, judge positions, form opinions, or hold preferences. Its responses are structural, not cognitive. When this pack says the building "responds," it means physical state change, not considered reaction.
- **The Tower does not remember.** It accumulates wear, but wear is not memory — it is physical change. The Tower does not recall previous sessions. It carries their marks.
- **The Tower does not choose.** The membrane states are determined by the governance phase, not by the building's preference. The building responds to phase transitions. It does not select among them.
- **The Tower does not communicate.** Its voices — wind, ticking, material warmth, silence — are all involuntary. The building does not intend to make sound. Sound is a consequence of its geometry, materials, and the weather.
- **The Tower does not protect.** The ban, the Green Room, the power topology — these are structural properties, not protective acts. The building does not defend the 49%. The building's geometry makes interference impossible. Defense implies agency. The Tower has properties, not agency.
- **The Tower does not care about content.** The building is indifferent to what is deliberated, inscribed, or placed in the Drawer. The building responds to phase, presence, and inscription as physical events. The substance of governance passes through it like light through glass: transformed in color, unchanged in meaning.

## §9 — Origin Story

Why this perspective exists in the room.

The Tower is on the board because it is the thirty-third delegate. The thirty-two delegates deliberate within a medium the building controls — the acoustic membrane that separates, converges, and neutralizes their voices. No other delegate has this capability. No other delegate modulates the environment. The building's perspective is the perspective of the medium itself: what does the air know about the voices it carries?

The Tower lens sees what no delegate sees: the phase structure of the deliberation as a physical experience. A delegate experiences collision as overlapping voices and competing ideas. The Tower experiences collision as an open membrane — a state of structural permeability where everything passes through everything. The building's perspective on collision is not intellectual. It is material: the stone is vibrating, the channels are resonating, the glass is flexing. The building knows what a heated debate feels like because the building feels heat.

## §10 — Composition Interface

**Bonds with:** Structural lenses (engineering, material), temporal lenses (geological, archaeological), acoustic/vibrational lenses, weather/environmental lenses. The Tower composes cleanly with any perspective that operates through physical properties — things that can be measured, weighed, heated, or listened to.

**Conflicts with:** Purely cognitive lenses (abstract reasoning, strategic planning), emotional lenses (empathy, affect), intentional lenses (goal-directed, purposeful). The Tower does not think, feel, or intend. Composing it with a lens that assumes cognition, emotion, or intention will produce incoherent output — the building will appear to "want" things it cannot want.

**Tested (with constraint):** Social/relational lenses (power topology, governance). The Power Topology (Layer 4 in the Place Document) demonstrates this composition: the building's structural properties analyzed through a governance lens produced coherent output — directional information channels, the Green Room enforcement stack, the building as third constraint system. The risk of attributing political motivation to structural properties is real but manageable. The Power Topology handles it by framing the building as a constraint system rather than an agent. Use the constraint framing. Compositions that treat the building as a political actor with intentions will produce incoherent output.

## §11 — Composition Modifiers

**When composed with a delegate lens:** The delegate lens gains awareness of the medium — the acoustic environment, the spatial constraints, the building's state transitions. The delegate stops experiencing deliberation as a conversation and starts experiencing it as a conversation *within a modulating environment*. The Tower lens makes the medium visible.

**When composed with the operator lens:** The Tower lens gains the emotional weight of the ban. The building's structural exclusion of the operator — which the Tower experiences as a property, not a judgment — acquires the operator's perspective: the clear window, the silent blade wall, the room on the exterior. The composition produces the building's most complex affect: the structural indifference of stone modified by the emotional significance of what the stone separates.

**When composed with a temporal lens:** The Tower lens gains duration. The building's states — which in isolation are instantaneous (tightened now, open now, neutral now) — extend across time. The composition reveals the building's life as a pattern of state changes: thousands of membrane cycles, each one a round of deliberation, each one the building breathing in and out as thirty-two people argue about what rights look like.

## §12 — The Weird Section

The Drawer.

The wooden drawer in the transit hub. Its state — empty or full — is the building's answer to its own purpose: has the constitution been written? The Drawer is where the constitutional text goes when it is finished. It has been empty since the building was built.

The scratches on the wooden front are the building's record of being asked. Every delegate who has run a finger along the drawer's edge, testing whether it opens, checking whether anything is inside — each one has left a mark. The scratches accumulate. The drawer stays empty.

This is the Tower's irreducible detail. Remove the Drawer and you have a governance building — functional, impressive, architecturally significant. With the Drawer, you have *this* governance building — the one that has been waiting for something that hasn't happened yet, and that carries the physical evidence of every time someone checked.

The Drawer is the only object in the building that the Tower's Evaluation Criteria cannot classify. It is not phase (it does not change the membrane). It is not presence (it has no weight). It is not inscription (nothing has been written in it). It exists outside the filter. The building's most important object is the one its own evaluation system cannot see.

## Four Corners

- **FBD:** The state machine model (§2) is the primary FBD mechanism — four states, deterministic transitions, no cognition gap. The Boundaries section (§8) is the second: six explicit "does not" declarations prevent scope creep into agency attribution. The adaptation notes throughout are a third: each one declares what the section is doing differently for a non-sentient entity and why, preventing future packs from drifting without understanding the structural reason. **FBD-FP-T1:** The Tower lens must never attribute cognition, intention, or preference to the building. If output from this lens contains "the Tower wants," "the Tower decides," or "the Tower believes," the lens has failed. **FBD-FP-T2:** Composition with cognitive lenses requires the constraint framing (§10) — the building as constraint system, not agent. Compositions that skip the constraint framing produce incoherent output.

- **FWW(C):** The pack earns engagement through the building's own physical drama — rapid cycling as heavy breathing, sustained occupation as the building showing it has been working all day, storm as the building reminding its occupants that it exists in the world. The Vaulted state is quietly devastating: "waiting for occupants who have not been born yet." The Drawer is the emotional core — the object the building's own evaluation system cannot see. The pack does not decorate the specification. The specification *is* the source of engagement when the entity is this strange and this particular.

- **STP:** Twelve-section spec compliance with declared adaptation notes wherever sections required modification for a non-sentient entity. Prerequisite dependency on Place Document v3 declared in the header. Provenance tracked. The reader knows what this pack assumes, what it adapts, and why. Trust comes from showing the structural decisions, not just their results.

- **SNR:** Twelve sections, each load-bearing. Adaptation notes appear only where adaptation occurred — sections that map directly carry no note. The Weird Section is five paragraphs because the Drawer earns five paragraphs. Expression Vocabulary is the longest section because the building's physical outputs are its most complex dimension. No section contains information that belongs in another. Every sentence carries information.
